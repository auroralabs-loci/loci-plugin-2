**Call graph:** ⚠ RISK

The two cleanup calls introduced by the refactor — `free(oadCCCDTable)` and `mq_close(hOadQ)` — each have ordering constraints that must be confirmed before writing:

- `mq_close(hOadQ)` is only valid when `mq_open` previously succeeded and returned a valid descriptor. `GATTServApp_RegisterService` is called near the end of `OAD_open`, well after `mq_open`. However, if an intermediate early return between `mq_open` and `GATTServApp_RegisterService` already calls `mq_close`, then `hOadQ` may be stale when the new cleanup runs — or the new cleanup may double-close it. The insertion point must be confirmed to be reachable only when `hOadQ` remains open.
- On TI BLE5 RTOS targets `mq_close` is a POSIX shim that may call `OsalPort_free` internally. Calling it with an invalid or already-closed descriptor can assert or corrupt the heap. Guard with a sentinel: initialize `hOadQ` to `(mqd_t)-1` at declaration and check `hOadQ != (mqd_t)-1` before calling `mq_close`.
- No indirect calls are expected for `free` or `mq_close` in standard RTOS shim usage, so no CFI hazard is anticipated on those calls themselves.
